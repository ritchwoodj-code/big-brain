# BIG BRAIN — Simple Guide
By Polaris Digital Studio | polarisdgtl.com

---

## What is it?

It's an upgrade for your AI. Once installed, instead of getting one generic
opinion when you describe a problem or decision, you get 15 different
executive-level thinking models reviewing it at once — and a clear verdict
at the end.

You don't learn anything new. You don't change how you work. You just talk
to Claude the same way you always did, and the answers get dramatically better
on anything that matters.

---

## Does it store anything? Is anything saved?

No. Nothing is stored anywhere outside your conversation.

Big Brain is a file that lives on your computer. When Claude reads it at the
start of a session, it loads into the conversation's memory — the same way
you loading up a document doesn't send it to a server. When the conversation
ends, it's gone from memory. The file stays on your computer unchanged.

No database. No external server. No data leaving your machine.

---

## Does it slow Claude down or use extra resources?

No. The file is about 2,500 words. Claude handles up to 200,000 words in
memory at once. Big Brain uses about 1% of that. You won't notice any
difference in speed.

---

## How do I use it?

Just talk to Claude normally. You don't need to type any special commands.

When you describe a decision, an idea, a plan, or something you're unsure
about — Big Brain kicks in automatically. Claude will ask you three short
questions to understand the situation, then run the analysis and give you
a clear verdict.

**Examples of things that trigger it automatically:**
- "I'm thinking about launching a new product. What do you think?"
- "Should I hire someone or keep doing this myself?"
- "Here's my plan for Q3. Am I missing anything?"
- "I need to decide between these two options..."

**Examples of things that won't trigger it** (because they don't need it):
- "Fix this bug in my code"
- "Write me a caption for this photo"
- "How do I install Python?"

---

## What can I use it for?

Anything where you need to make a real decision before committing time or money:

- Validating a new business idea before building it
- Reviewing a launch plan before you go public
- Deciding on a hire, a partnership, or a pricing change
- Thinking through a tech architecture before you build it
- Stress-testing a strategy before you pitch it

It's most valuable for decisions that are hard to reverse — where getting
it wrong costs weeks or months, not minutes.

---

## What does it NOT do?

- It doesn't talk to your customers. It asks the right questions about them.
- It doesn't write code or build things. It tells you what to build and in what order.
- It doesn't guarantee success. A GREEN verdict means the logic is sound — not that it'll work.
- It doesn't replace your judgment. It sharpens it.

## How do I install it?

**If you use Claude Code:**
1. Open terminal
2. Run: `notepad C:\Users\[YourName]\.claude\CLAUDE.md`
   (Mac: `nano ~/.claude/CLAUDE.md`)
3. Scroll to the very bottom
4. Copy everything from the file `install/claude-code/big-brain-claude.md`
5. Paste at the bottom. Save.

Done. Every Claude Code session from that point forward has Big Brain active.
You never touch it again.

**If you use Claude.ai, ChatGPT, or any other AI:**
Copy the file `install/universal/system-prompt.txt` and paste it at the very
top of a new conversation. It works for that session.

---

## How do I know it's working?

Open a fresh Claude Code session and describe a decision you're thinking about.
Claude should ask you three questions before responding:
1. What specific decision are you trying to make?
2. What would success look like in 90 days?
3. What's the one thing you're most uncertain about?

If those three questions come back, it's working.

## Optional shortcuts (you don't need these, but they exist)

If you're in a hurry and want a fast answer without the full review:
Type: `check [your question]`
You get a signal (pass/fail/conditional), the key tension, and one action — in under 200 words.

If you know what you want to build and just need the right order:
Type: `build [your task]`
You get the one blocking step, a sequence of 4-6 steps, and the first thing to produce.
