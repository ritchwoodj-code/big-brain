## Why a License Exists

Big Brain is not open-source. The value in this system is not the list of
personas — those are public knowledge. The value is:

1. **The routing logic.** Which personas to pair, which to use as adversarial
   counterweights, and how decision type maps to thinking model. This took
   two rounds of deep research synthesis to build correctly.

2. **The combination rules.** The mono-persona governance warning, the staged
   review protocol, and the exit criteria per persona are not obvious. Most
   people who read about Bezos's PR/FAQ or Musk's first principles use them
   in isolation and wonder why they don't work as well as advertised. The
   combination is the product.

3. **The adoption artifacts.** The specific documents each persona requires
   (PRR, downside memo, PR/FAQ, takt board, eval suite) turn a thinking
   framework into a working system. That translation is the IP.

4. **The eval suite.** 5 canonical test cases with expected signal outputs
   mean you can verify that Big Brain is working correctly and detect when
   a system prompt update has degraded quality. No other similar framework
   ships with a QA suite.

A competitor who reads this repo can see what Big Brain does. They cannot
easily replicate the research synthesis, the routing calibration, or the
version-to-version improvement track record that builds up over time.
